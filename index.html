Interesting Facts about Camels:

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat..
  • Camels have humps that let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months.

Interesting Facts about Chameleons:

  • Chameleons are repitiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of commmunication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Interesting Facts about Pigeons:

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Interesting Facts about Teddy Bears:

  • The Teddy Bear was named after President Theodore Roosevelt after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  • Inspired by a cartoon featuring the event, shopkeeper Morris Michtom and his wife Rose made a stuffed bear with a sign 'Teddy's bear' and left it in their shop window.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Interesting Facts about Panda:

  • Pandas have extra digit on their paws that functions like a thumb which helps them tear bamboo.
  • Bamboo makes up 99% of a panda's diet with the remain 1% made up of eggs, small animals, carrion, and pumpkin, kidney beans, wheat, and domestic pig food.
  • Pandas hava strong jaws to crush bamboo and a special lining to protect its throat from splinters once the bamboo is swallowed.

Interesting Facts about Flying Cicada:

  • The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar, in that much of it consists of empty, air-filled spaces that act like a resonating chamber and amplify the sound they generate.
  • Cicadas live from 4-17 years and spend most of their lives underground as larvae, followed by a short adulthood, typically from 2-6 weeks..
  • Cicada nymphs drink sap from the xylem of various species of trees while common folklore indicates that adults do not eat but acutally drink plant sap using their sucking mouth parts.
